# Commercial License

## Why AGPL?

GID CLI is licensed under AGPL-3.0 to ensure that improvements to the core
tool benefit the entire community. If you modify GID and provide it as a
service, you must open-source your modifications.

## When Do You Need a Commercial License?

You need a commercial license if:

1. **Proprietary Products**: You want to include GID in a closed-source product
2. **SaaS Offerings**: You're building a hosted service using GID
3. **No Open Source**: You cannot or do not want to open-source your code
4. **Enterprise Needs**: You need additional support, SLAs, or custom features

## What Does a Commercial License Include?

- Permission to use GID in proprietary software
- No requirement to open-source derivative works
- Priority support and bug fixes
- Custom feature development (Enterprise tier)
- Legal indemnification (Enterprise tier)

## FAQ

**Q: Can I use GID for free in my company?**
A: Yes, if you comply with AGPL-3.0 (open-source any modifications and
   services built on GID).

**Q: What if I just use GID CLI as a development tool?**
A: Internal use as a development tool is generally fine under AGPL.
   The AGPL's copyleft provisions apply when you distribute the software
   or provide it as a network service to others.

**Q: Can I contribute to GID and still get a commercial license?**
A: Yes! Contributors are welcome and may receive discounted commercial licenses.
